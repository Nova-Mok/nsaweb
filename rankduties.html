Rank Duties & Tasks

The following descriptions are provided to articulate the specific purposes of each division within the NSA. Every division and its individual members play an integral role in the daily operations of the NSA. It is crucial for all members to have a thorough grasp of the duties and tasks associated with each division. Please be aware that adherence to these responsibilities while at the base is mandatory. Non-compliance may lead to demotion or, in severe cases, dismissal.

Standard Division

Trainees must complete Recruit Training.
Manage the Front Desk (FTF) duties.
Display welcome messages for all visitors at the base.
Security Division

Security members are mandated to undergo security training.
Responsibilities include overseeing the security area.
Promote welcoming messages and ensure the overall safety of the base
Trainer Division

Trainer must undergo Trainer Training.
Conduct training sessions for new recruits.
Provide guidance and support during the training process.
High Rank

High Rank members are mandated to complete High Rank Training.
Authorized to make promotions up to the Trainer.
Assist Standard – Trainers if they require assistance.
Legal Intelligence

Authorized to make promotions up to the High Ranks.
Supervise the Trainer divisions and coordinate with High Ranks on their tasks.
Ensure fair treatment of lower-ranking members by everyone.
Agency Affairs

Authorized to make promotions up to Legal Intelligence.
Responsible for overseeing basic station operations, including front desk, security, and training.
Government

Required to man the Help Desk during their base presence.
Authorized to make promotions up to Agency Affairs.
Responsible for welcoming and touring new members.
OOA

Authorized to make promotions up to Government.
Monitoring the performance and activities of Government members.
Authorized to demote workers until High Rank Division.
Diplomats

Authorized to make promotions up to OOA.
Authorized to command Clear the hallway.
Authorized to demote workers until Agency Affairs.
Top Management

Oversee the promotion logs from the Standard Division up to Government.
Authorized to make promotions up to Diplomats.
Authorized to demote and dismiss worker Government members.
Ensuring that agency policies and rules are followed and implemented.
Elite Board

Authorized to conduct promotions up to Top Management.
This rank should report to the Leadership (5iC) about the base’s updates/issues.
Authorized to make announcements on base.
Eligible to apply for Trial Elective (if applicable).
Trial Elective

Authorized to conduct promotions up to Top Management.
Required to successfully complete the Trial Elective program for promotion eligibility.
